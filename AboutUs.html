Alankrita Pandey's venture of starting Titli Foundation would not have taken off without the incessant support of her childhood friends
- Devansh Agarwal, Vaibhav Tripathi, and Chinmay Singh.

Incepted against the backdrop of a society that has stigmatized menstruation for years and has spun a cocoon of shame around it, embarking on the journey of starting an NGO to #BreakTheTaboo in a country that suffocates menstruators with its heavy superstitions was very daunting.

Having witnessed and experienced appalling social perils herself, the cause and desire to break the taboo and fight against period poverty spoke to her heart and overpowered every little apprehension.

The metamorphosis of Titli Foundation has not only been phenomenal but inspirational as well. Stemming from the need to help the underprivileged have a safer and better menstrual experience, Titli has grown into a second home for its members and all those who seek information about the menstrual cup.

Why the Cup?

At Titli, we envision a world where no menstruator has to compromise on menstrual hygiene for any possible reason. When we promote a menstrual cup we aim to provide the marginalized communities with a pocket-friendly and reusable alternative.
