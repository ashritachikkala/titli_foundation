Our Vision

We hope to break the taboo around menstruation by promoting and educating the masses about menstrual health and hygiene, and donating menstrual cups, a more pocket-friendly and sustainable alternative to other sanitary products.

Our Mission

Out of a total of 40 crore menstruating women in India, less than 20% use sanitary pads. We, at Titli Foundation, aspire to change this statistic, and help the underprivileged overcome the menstrual hurdles of inaccessibility, unaffordability, as well as lack of awareness and hygiene.

Our Reach

Rooted in Ayodhya, Titli Foundation has spread its wings with over 204 members across India, and having active groundwork teams host drives to donate menstrual cups and pads in Ayodhya, Lucknow, Pune, Mumbai, Delhi and Bangalore. Efficient Use

Menstrual cups can hold more than an ounce of liquid, roughly twice the capacity of a premium quality pad or tampon, and can go up to 12 hours before emptying.

Pocket & Eco-Friendly

Being reusable, a menstrual cup can last through long durations of time (as long as 10 years!), leading to less environmental waste, and less costs over time.

A Safer Alternative

Since menstrual cups collect, rather than absorbing blood, it prevents the risk of getting Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS), which is usually caused by usage of super-absorbent tampons.
